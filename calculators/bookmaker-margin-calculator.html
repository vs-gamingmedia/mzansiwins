Understanding the Bookmaker Margin

The bookmaker margin (also called vig, juice, or overround) is the built-in advantage that ensures sportsbooks profit over time. In a perfectly fair market, the implied probabilities of all outcomes sum to exactly 100%. In reality, they sum to more - the excess is the bookmaker's profit margin.

How It Works

In a fair coin toss (50/50), both sides should be priced at 2.00. But a bookmaker might offer 1.90 on both sides:

  • Implied probability: (1/1.90) + (1/1.90) = 52.6% + 52.6% = 105.3%
  • Margin = 105.3% - 100% = 5.3%
  • This 5.3% is the bookmaker's edge on every bet placed in this market

What is a Good Margin?

  • 2-4% - Excellent. Sharp bookmakers on main markets
  • 4-6% - Good. Standard for major SA bookmakers on popular sports
  • 6-10% - Average. Common on less popular markets
  • 10%+ - High. Niche markets, novelty bets, or less competitive bookmakers

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a bookmaker margin?

The bookmaker margin (also called vig, juice, or overround) is the difference between the true probabilities and the implied probabilities in the odds. It represents the bookmaker's profit edge. A margin of 5% means the bookmaker expects to keep R5 of every R100 wagered in that market.

How do I calculate the bookmaker margin?

Add up the implied probability of each outcome (1 divided by the decimal odds for each). If the total is 105%, the margin is 5%. This calculator does it automatically for 2-way and 3-way markets.

What is a good bookmaker margin?

A margin of 2-4% is excellent, typically found on major markets at competitive bookmakers. 4-6% is standard for South African bookmakers on popular sports. Anything above 10% is high and means poor value for bettors.

Which SA bookmaker has the lowest margins?

Margins vary by sport and market. Generally, Betway and Sportingbet offer competitive margins on international soccer, while Hollywoodbets often has sharp odds on South African sports. Always compare margins on the specific market you are betting on.
