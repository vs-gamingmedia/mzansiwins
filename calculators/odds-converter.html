How to Convert Betting Odds

South African sportsbooks like Hollywoodbets, Betway, and Apexbets primarily display decimal odds, but understanding all three formats gives you an edge when comparing markets internationally.

Decimal Odds (Most Common in SA)

Decimal odds show the total return for every R1 staked. For example, odds of 2.50 mean a R100 bet returns R250 (R150 profit + R100 stake). This is the default format at every major South African bookmaker.

Fractional Odds (UK Style)

Written as 3/2, 5/1, etc. The first number is the profit relative to the second number. So 3/2 means R3 profit for every R2 staked, which equals 2.50 in decimal.

American Odds (Moneyline)

Positive numbers (+150) show profit on a R100 stake. Negative numbers (-200) show how much you need to stake for R100 profit. Useful when following US sports markets.

Implied Probability Explained

The implied probability tells you what chance the bookmaker's odds represent. Odds of 2.00 imply a 50% chance. By comparing this to your own assessment, you can identify value bets where the bookmaker has underestimated an outcome's likelihood.

Tips for SA Punters

  • Most SA bookmakers use decimal odds by default, so start there
  • Compare odds across Hollywoodbets, Betway, and Sportingbet to find the best price
  • Use implied probability to spot value - if you think a team has a 50% chance but the odds imply only 40%, that is a value bet
  • International bookmakers sometimes display fractional or American odds, so knowing how to convert is essential

Frequently Asked Questions

What are decimal odds?

Decimal odds show the total return (including your stake) for every R1 bet. For example, decimal odds of 2.50 mean a R100 bet returns R250. Most South African bookmakers like Hollywoodbets, Betway, and Apexbets use decimal odds as their default format.

How do I convert fractional odds to decimal?

Divide the first number by the second and add 1. For example, 3/2 becomes (3 divided by 2) + 1 = 2.50 in decimal odds. This converter does it instantly for you.

What is implied probability in betting?

Implied probability is the percentage chance of an outcome that the bookmaker's odds represent. Calculated as 1 divided by the decimal odds, multiplied by 100. Odds of 2.00 imply a 50% probability. Compare this to your own estimate to find value.

Which odds format do South African bookmakers use?

All major South African bookmakers - including Hollywoodbets, Betway, Sportingbet, and Supabets - use decimal odds as their default format. You can usually switch to fractional in your account settings.
