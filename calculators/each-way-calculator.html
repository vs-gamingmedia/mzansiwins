Understanding Each Way Bets

An each-way bet is really two bets in one: a win bet and a place bet. It is most commonly used in horse racing but also applies to golf and other sports with large fields. Your total stake is double the amount you enter, as you are backing both win and place.

How Place Terms Work

The place portion is paid at a fraction of the win odds. Common place terms in South African racing:

  • 1/4 odds - the most common. Used in races with 8+ runners
  • 1/5 odds - smaller fields with fewer place positions
  • 1/3 odds - handicaps and large-field races

When to Use Each Way

Each-way bets work best on longer-priced selections (8.00+). At short prices, the place return often does not cover your total stake. The rule of thumb: only bet each-way if the odds are 5/1 (6.00 decimal) or higher.

SA Horse Racing Context

South African racing runs at tracks like Turffontein, Kenilworth, Greyville, and Scottsville. Most SA bookmakers offer each-way betting on local and international racing. Hollywoodbets is particularly popular for horse racing with competitive each-way terms.

Quick Example

A R50 each-way bet at 8.00 with 1/4 odds place terms costs R100 total. If the horse wins you collect both parts. If it only places, you still get a return on the place portion.
