How Arbitrage Betting Works

Arbitrage betting (arbing) exploits odds differences between bookmakers. When the combined implied probability of all outcomes drops below 100%, a guaranteed profit exists regardless of the result. This calculator finds those opportunities and tells you exactly how to split your stakes.

The Math Behind Arbitrage

For a two-outcome event, add the implied probabilities: (1/OddsA) + (1/OddsB). If the total is less than 1.00 (100%), there is an arbitrage opportunity.

  • Bookmaker A: Team X at 2.10 (implied: 47.6%)
  • Bookmaker B: Team Y at 2.05 (implied: 48.8%)
  • Total: 96.4% - that is 3.6% guaranteed profit

Finding Arbs in South Africa

With 36 licensed SA bookmakers, there are plenty of odds to compare. Common arb sources:

  • Pre-match vs live - odds sometimes lag on one bookmaker when a market shifts
  • Lesser-known markets - player props, corners, and niche sports have wider margins
  • Different bookmakers - compare Hollywoodbets, Betway, Sportingbet, and Supabets regularly

Risks and Limitations

  • Bookmakers may limit or close accounts of arbers
  • Odds can change between placing both sides
  • Minimum stakes and maximum payouts affect profitability
  • Always use multiple bookmaker accounts to spread risk

Frequently Asked Questions

What is arbitrage betting?

Arbitrage betting involves placing bets on all possible outcomes of an event across different bookmakers, guaranteeing a profit regardless of the result. This is possible when the combined implied probability across bookmakers is less than 100%.

Is arbitrage betting legal in South Africa?

Yes, arbitrage betting is completely legal in South Africa. You are simply placing bets at different bookmakers at the odds they offer. However, bookmakers may limit your account if they suspect consistent arbing.

How much profit can you make from arbitrage?

Typical arbitrage opportunities offer 1-5% profit. On a R1,000 total stake, that means R10-R50 guaranteed profit per arb. The profit is small per bet but risk-free, and it adds up over many bets.

How do I find arbitrage opportunities in SA?

Compare odds across multiple South African bookmakers for the same event. Focus on markets with many competitors - such as match winner in soccer or rugby. Tools and calculators like this one help identify when the combined implied probability drops below 100%.
