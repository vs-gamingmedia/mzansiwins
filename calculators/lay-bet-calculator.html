What is a Lay Bet?

A lay bet is the opposite of a normal (back) bet. Instead of betting on something to happen, you bet against it. When you lay a bet on an exchange, you are acting as the bookmaker. If the outcome does not happen, you win the backer's stake (minus commission). If it does happen, you pay out.

Understanding Liability

Your liability is the maximum amount you could lose on a lay bet. It equals your lay stake multiplied by (lay odds - 1). This amount is held by the exchange until the bet is settled.

For example, laying R100 at 3.00:

  • Liability = R100 x (3.00 - 1) = R200
  • If the outcome does not happen: you win R100 minus commission
  • If the outcome happens: you lose R200

Lay Betting Uses

  • Matched betting - pair back bets at bookmakers with lay bets on exchanges to extract free bet value
  • Trading - back and then lay (or vice versa) to lock in profit as odds move
  • Hedging - lay off existing bets to reduce risk
  • Opposing favourites - if you think the market has overvalued a selection, lay it

Exchange Commission

Betting exchanges charge commission (typically 5%) on winning lay bets. This calculator includes the commission in all calculations so you see the true profit and loss figures.

Frequently Asked Questions

A lay bet means betting against an outcome happening. Instead of backing a team to win, you bet that they will not win. If the team does not win (they lose or draw), you win. If they win, you pay out. It is the opposite of a normal bet.

Liability is the maximum amount you could lose on a lay bet. It equals your lay stake multiplied by (lay odds minus 1). For example, laying R50 at odds of 4.00 means a liability of R150.

Most betting exchanges charge 5% commission on net winning bets. Some exchanges offer reduced commission for high-volume bettors. This calculator defaults to 5% but you can adjust it.

Betting exchange access in South Africa is limited compared to the UK. Some international exchanges accept SA residents, but check the terms and conditions. Many SA bettors use lay betting concepts through hedge betting across different bookmakers.
