How Accumulator Bets Work

An accumulator (also called a multi bet, parlay, or acca) combines two or more selections into a single bet. All selections must win for the bet to pay out. The odds of each selection are multiplied together, creating much higher potential returns - but with higher risk.

How Combined Odds Are Calculated

The odds multiply together. Three selections at 1.80, 2.00, and 1.60:

  • Combined odds = 1.80 x 2.00 x 1.60 = 5.76
  • A R100 bet returns R576 if all three win
  • But if even one selection loses, the entire bet is lost

Accumulator Strategy Tips

Bookmakers love accumulators because the house edge multiplies with each leg. Here is how to use them wisely:

  • Keep it short - 2-4 selections is the sweet spot. Each leg adds risk exponentially
  • Avoid heavy favourites only - combining five 1.20 odds selections looks safe but the return is poor relative to the risk
  • Consider same-game multis - many SA bookmakers like Betway and Hollywoodbets offer same-game accumulator builders
  • Look for acca insurance - some SA bookmakers refund your stake if one leg lets you down
