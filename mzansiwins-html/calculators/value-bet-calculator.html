What is a Value Bet?

A value bet occurs when the bookmaker's odds imply a lower probability than you believe the true probability to be. Finding value consistently is the single most important skill in profitable sports betting. It does not matter if any individual bet wins or loses - over hundreds of bets, value betting is mathematically guaranteed to profit.

The Expected Value Formula

EV = (Probability x Profit) - ((1 - Probability) x Stake)

If you find a bet with positive expected value (+EV), placing it is the correct decision long-term, even if the individual bet might lose.

Example

A bookmaker offers 2.50 (implied 40%) on a match outcome, but your analysis suggests the true probability is 45%:

  • Value = (0.45 x 2.50) - 1 = 0.125 or +12.5%
  • This means for every R100 bet, you expect to profit R12.50 on average over many bets

How to Find Value in SA Betting Markets

  • Specialise - become an expert in one league or sport. South African soccer (PSL), rugby, or cricket are good starting points
  • Compare odds - check prices across Hollywoodbets, Betway, Sportingbet, and other SA bookmakers
  • Look for biases - popular teams often have shorter odds than they should because of public money
  • Track your bets - record every bet and compare your predicted probabilities with actual outcomes
